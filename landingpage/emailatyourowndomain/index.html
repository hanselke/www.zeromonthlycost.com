Guide to getting email at your own Domain

Having a custom business email address is very important as part of your branding strategy.
In today's Internet enviroment it also acts as your your online identity, essentially your passport that's used to login to most websites & apps you use.

So, where do we begin?

What you need to know

    Business email should never be down.

    While email servers crashing and getting restored within a few hours without losing data isnt a big deal, email is a critical communication tool. You also need it to sign up for stuff and recover forgotten passwords.
    It's a huge pain and email failure should just not be tolerated today.
    What you need is a vendor that runs email in a highly available infrastructure that just works. There should be at least 2GB of space per user to realistically not have to delete email for businesses not sending large files via email regularly.
    Then next, what you should have come to expect from modern software tools:

      Use it with any mail client that you are using now
      An easy to use web interface

    What you want to pay?

    Besides Zoho Mail, there are no other free email services that host your custom domain. Both Google Apps and Microsoft 365 have stopped their official free programs and closed up loopholes that used to be available.

    There are other vendors that are reputable and fits the requirements like Rackspace Email ($2/mth/usr), Atmail($2/mth/usr) or fastmail ($40+/yr/domain).
    If you are going to pay a monthly subscription fee, you might as well get more bang for your buck with either Google Apps for Work or Microsoft 365.
